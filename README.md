14.	Recommendations
Based on the findings of this study, the following recommendations are proposed to improve logistics performance and cost efficiency.
Optimize Routes with Frequent Delays
Routes that consistently experience late deliveries should be reviewed and redesigned. Management should analyze travel distances, traffic conditions, customer waiting times, and road quality to determine the most efficient routes and realistic delivery schedules.
Implement Driver Performance Monitoring Systems
A formal driver performance monitoring system should be introduced to track metrics such as on-time delivery rate, average delivery time, fuel efficiency, and safety incidents. Drivers with strong performance should be recognized, while those with lower performance should receive additional coaching and support.
Reduce Fuel Consumption Through Efficient Planning
Because fuel represents the largest operational cost, the company should adopt strategies to minimize unnecessary consumption. These strategies include reducing idle time, balancing loads, improving route planning, and encouraging fuel-efficient driving practices.
Schedule Preventive Maintenance for Trucks
Preventive maintenance should be carried out regularly to reduce unexpected breakdowns and costly repairs. Trucks with recurring maintenance issues should be closely monitored and, where necessary, replaced to maintain reliability and lower long-term costs.
Use Dashboards for Real-Time Monitoring
Management should continue using Power BI dashboards to monitor KPIs such as on-time delivery rate, fuel cost, maintenance cost, and fleet utilization. Real-time visibility enables faster decision-making and supports continuous improvement.
Balance Fleet Utilization
Trip assignments should be distributed more evenly across the fleet to avoid overusing certain trucks while leaving others underutilized. Balanced utilization helps extend vehicle life and improves resource efficiency.
Strengthen Safety Training: Drivers with higher incident rates should receive additional safety training and closer supervision. Improved safety performance will reduce accidents, repair costs, and service disruptions.
